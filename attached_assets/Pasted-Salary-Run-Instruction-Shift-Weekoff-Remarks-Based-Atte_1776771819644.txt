Salary Run Instruction — Shift / Weekoff / Remarks Based Attendance Policy
Objective

Build the attendance and salary processing logic using shift and week off master data.
This logic will be used as the base for salary run, so the final report must clearly show applied shift rules, week off details, late deductions, overtime, and remarks for each employee.

Data Source Mapping
Shift details source: /shifts
Week off source: /weekoffs

These two sources must be used as the primary reference for attendance interpretation and salary calculation.

Core Rule

For each employee, determine:

Assigned shift from /shifts
Applicable week off from /weekoffs
Attendance in/out punches
Late minutes
Overtime minutes/hours
Half day / off day handling
Final remarks explaining how the calculation was derived
Shift Policy Master
Shift Category	Standard Working Time	Late Rule	OT Rule	Remarks Rule
Regular Shift	8:00 AM - 5:00 PM	Late after 8:15 AM	OT after 5:30 PM	Use normal late and OT calculation
Reception Shift	8:30 AM - 5:30 PM	Late after 8:45 AM	OT after 6:00 PM	Use reception-specific late and OT cut-off
Night Shift	8:00 PM - 5:00 AM	No standard late rule defined	Fixed OT up to 8:00 AM	Add 3 hours OT if valid; hourly punches mandatory
Kitchen Shift	Varies by day	Late after 15 minutes from shift start	OT based on assigned rule / extended time	Shift timing may vary by day, so compare against actual assigned shift
Flexible Shift	Flexible hours	No late deduction	OT only if total hours exceed 9.30 hours	No fixed start-time late deduction
Half Day Shift	Usually up to 1:00 PM or as assigned	Apply only if half-day shift has fixed reporting time	OT only if explicitly allowed	Must be shown clearly as half day in report
Common Attendance Policies
1. Late Calculation
Late deduction starts after 15 minutes grace unless shift-specific rule overrides it.
Example:
Shift start: 8:00 AM
Employee in: 8:18 AM
Late = 3 minutes
For Regular Shift, grace ends at 8:15 AM
For Reception Shift, grace ends at 8:45 AM
For Kitchen Shift, late applies after 15 minutes from assigned shift start
For Flexible Shift, no late deduction
2. Overtime Calculation
OT should be calculated only after 30 minutes threshold
Example:
Shift end: 5:00 PM
Punch out: 5:45 PM
OT = 15 minutes
For Regular Shift, OT starts after 5:30 PM
For Reception Shift, OT starts after 6:00 PM
For Flexible Shift, OT applies only if total working hours exceed 9.30 hours
For Night Shift, OT is fixed 3 hours up to 8:00 AM, subject to punch validation
For Kitchen Shift, OT must follow assigned kitchen shift rules and actual worked duration
3. Night Shift Validation

For Night Shift employees:

Assigned shift: 8:00 PM - 5:00 AM
Actual off time considered up to 8:00 AM
Add 3 hours OT
But hourly punch validation is mandatory
If employee misses required hourly punch:
deduct 1 or 2 hours from 3-hour OT as per rule
Report remarks must clearly say:
3 hrs OT added
or OT reduced due to missed hourly punch
4. Week Off Handling
Weekly off must come from /weekoffs
If attendance exists on a scheduled week off:
mark clearly as Worked on Week Off
show worked hours
show whether OT / extra pay rule applies
If no attendance on week off:
mark as Week Off
Week off day must always appear in report, even if no punch exists
5. Half Day Handling

If assigned day is half day:

show shift as half day
compare attendance only against half-day schedule
do not treat half-day planned exit as early out
remarks should clearly show:
Half Day Shift
Half Day Worked
Half Day Absent
whichever is applicable
6. Lunch / Grace Policy
Lunch out adjustment: add 10 minutes
Final checkout grace: 5 minutes
These adjustments must be considered before final late / OT result is frozen
If implemented, report remarks must clearly mention:
Lunch grace applied
Checkout grace applied
Calculation Flow

For each employee and date:

Read employee shift from /shifts
Read employee week off from /weekoffs
Identify whether the date is:
Working day
Week off
Half day
Read first in-punch and last out-punch
Apply shift-specific late rule
Apply shift-specific OT rule
Apply grace rules
Apply special logic for:
Night Shift
Flexible Shift
Kitchen Shift
Half day
Week off work
Generate final row with proper remarks
Use this final output for salary run
Mandatory Report Output

The final report must be clear enough for payroll verification.
Every row must contain the following columns:

Column Name	Description
Employee ID / Name	Employee identification
Date	Attendance date
Shift Category	Shift type from /shifts
Shift Time	Assigned shift timing
Week Off Status	From /weekoffs
First In	First valid punch in
Last Out	Last valid punch out
Worked Hours	Net worked hours
Late Minutes	Final late minutes after grace
OT Minutes / Hours	Final OT after threshold rules
Day Type	Working Day / Week Off / Half Day / Absent
Remarks	Proper explanation of applied rule
Mandatory Remarks Format

Remarks must be meaningful and payroll-friendly.
Do not leave remarks vague.
Remarks should explain exactly what happened.

Use remarks like:

Regular Shift - Late by 12 mins
Regular Shift - OT 1 hr 15 mins
Reception Shift - Within grace
Night Shift - 3 hrs OT added
Night Shift - 1 hr OT deducted due to missed hourly punch
Week Off - Not worked
Week Off - Worked and eligible for OT
Half Day Shift - Completed
Flexible Shift - No late deduction
Flexible Shift - OT applied after 9.30 hrs
Kitchen Shift - Shift varied as assigned
Lunch grace applied
Checkout grace applied

Remarks must always make the salary logic auditable.

Important Implementation Notes
Do not hardcode one common rule for all employees
Always derive shift logic from /shifts
Always derive off-day logic from /weekoffs
Shift-specific rules must override generic rules
Report must include all dates relevant for salary run, including:
present days
week off days
half days
absences if required by payroll
Final report must be readable by HR/payroll without checking raw punch logs
